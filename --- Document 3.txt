# Standard Operating Procedure (SOP): Classroom Safety Sweeps

## Purpose
This document establishes the mandatory protocol for conducting daily morning health and environmental safety sweeps. Consistent execution of this procedure ensures full alignment with state licensing standards and guarantees a hazard-free environment before students enter the facility.

## Scope
This procedure applies to all lead educators, childcare aids, and floaters assigned to active classroom spaces.

---

## 1. Pre-Sweep Readiness Check
Perform these foundational tasks before conducting the physical environmental sweep:
* **Verify** the classroom emergency binder is present on the primary wall mount.
* **Confirm** the mobile first-aid toolkit is fully stocked with unexpired supplies.
* **Ensure** your employee identification badge is visible.

## 2. Classroom Environmental Sweep Matrix
Execute the daily inspection following the visual matrix below. Every item must achieve a "Pass" status before children are permitted to enter the classroom space.

| Inspection Area | Specific Hazard Target | Required Compliance Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Electrical Outlets** | Exposed wiring, missing safety plugs | **Insert** plastic safety caps into all unused electrical outlets. |
| **Walkways & Floors** | Tripping hazards, moisture, loose rugs | **Clear** all debris from main pathways. **Mop** and dry wet spots immediately. |
| **Sanitization Station** | Unlocked cleaning chemicals, low soap | **Lock** all chemical cleaning agents inside the designated overhead cabinet. |
| **Toy Repositories** | Cracked plastics, sharp edges, small parts | **Remove** damaged items from the floor and place them in the maintenance bin. |

## 3. Incident Reporting Protocol
If any area fails the inspection matrix and cannot be immediately corrected by the on-site aid:
1. **Mark** the specific item as "FAIL" on the daily paper logging sheet.
2. **Isolate** the hazardous area using physical furniture boundaries if necessary.
3. **Notify** the center director via the internal communications portal within 15 minutes.
4. **Log** a formal maintenance request tracking number for the facilities repair crew.
